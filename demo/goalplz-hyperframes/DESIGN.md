# Goalplz HyperFrames Design

## Style Prompt

Quiet developer-tool demo on a dark terminal canvas. The piece should feel like a focused README asset: understated, readable, and practical. Avoid launch-video gloss; show the product behavior plainly.

## Colors

- Background: `#0d1117`
- Panel: `#111827`
- Elevated panel: `#172033`
- Text: `#e5e7eb`
- Muted text: `#9ca3af`
- Border: `#2d3748`
- Goal blue: `#60a5fa`
- Verified green: `#34d399`
- Warning amber: `#fbbf24`

## Typography

- Display and terminal voice: `Cascadia Mono`, `Consolas`, monospace
- Body fallback: `Segoe UI`, system UI, sans-serif
- Use tabular numbers and ligatures off for code-like text.

## Motion

- Use small terminal-like reveals and short fades.
- Keep motion mostly horizontal: prompt enters, goal resolves.
- No decorative ambient motion.

## What NOT to Do

- No generic purple-blue hero gradient, neon glow, or decorative grid.
- No oversized marketing headline that hides the product behavior.
- No floating chips, token clouds, badges, or ornamental rings.
- No jump cuts, empty frames, or long holds with no visual change.
- No invented capabilities beyond request-to-goal compilation, constraints, verification, and pause conditions.
